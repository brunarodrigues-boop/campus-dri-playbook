PHASE 2 · SKILLS

Known Student Learning Anti-Patterns

Learning anti-patterns are student behaviours that look productive but do not lead to actual learning. (Last updated 4/12/2026)

Anti-Pattern Reference

Anti-PatternWhat It Looks Like
Rushing through contentMoving through lessons or problems unusually fast without engaging with the material.
Skipping explanations after errorsGetting a question wrong and immediately moving on without reading or watching the explanation.
Guessing / clicking through answersSelecting answers randomly or rapidly without reading the question, often visible as very short response times.
Skipping instructional materialBypassing videos, worked examples, or lesson content and jumping straight to the quiz or practice problems.
Idling / off-taskLong gaps of inactivity during a learning session, indicating the student is not actively working.
Gaming the systemExploiting the platform to earn points without real learning — e.g., retaking mastered content on easy mode or repeating low-level exercises for XP.
Ramp-up connection: You will identify these exact anti-patterns in real student sessions as part of your Part 1 ramp-up task on StudyReel / TimeBack Flow / Vault.

PHASE 2 · RAMP-UP

Ramp-Up Task

This task has two parts. Connect theory to product reality through hands-on student review.

Part 1: Anti-Pattern Identification (Warm-Up)

Review learning sessions for 7 students on StudyReel or TimeBack Flow/Vault. For each student, watch their sessions and identify which learning anti-patterns are present — such as rushing through content, skipping explanations, gaming the system, or idling.

After you submit your observations, you can compare your findings with the academic team’s actual findings.

Part 2: Full Deep Dive Analysis

Analyse 10 student cases from the Winter MAP growth cycle. For each student, you will see their performance metrics (XP, minutes, growth, retakes) and must write a structured analysis covering:

  • Diagnosis — What went wrong and why? Trace every issue back to an adult decision or system failure.
  • Academic Intervention — What placement, curriculum, or system change should be made? The fix must be scalable — no 1-on-1 tutoring.
  • Motivational Strategy — What motivational model would you implement for this student?

Your analysis is checked against Alpha’s academic standards before you can see the answer key. Use the Help & Resources panel to access TimeBack Dash, QuickSight dashboards, StudyReel, and other sources as you investigate each case.

PHASE 2 · CAMPUS DRI

Deep Dive Process

A structured written analysis of a student’s performance produced when recurring issues are detected. Your primary deliverable as Campus DRI.

When to Write a Deep Dive

  • Student is flagged in the Student Deep Dives Dashboard
  • Two or more critical anti-patterns observed in the same week
  • A guide requests a review or flags a student as struggling
  • Student is stuck in a pattern of repeated low performance across sessions

Structure

SectionWhat Goes Here
At a GlanceKey metrics and overall status
Issues Identified1–3 specific problems with evidence references (session dates, StudyReel timestamps)
Detailed ObservationsSupporting evidence grouped by category
Root CauseArea → Observed Issue → Likely Root Cause. Trace back to an adult decision or system failure.
RecommendationsOne per issue — specific, actionable, directed at Guide or Campus DRI
Action PlanWho does what by when
Use the template: The Deep Dive template is in the campus-dri-playbook resources. Never write one from scratch.

PHASE 3 · CAMPUS DRI

Triangle Evaluation

Your structured assessment of guide quality. Three pillars, conducted once per session.

The Three Pillars

Pillar 1: Guide Survey

A structured survey sent to the guide once per session. Covers student engagement quality, intervention moments, and guide self-assessment. Read responses before delivering feedback.

Pillar 2: Head of Campus DRI Review

Your direct data review of the guide’s students. Did their cohort improve? Were flagged issues addressed? Were coaching points delivered? This is the quantitative backbone of the evaluation.

Pillar 3: Student Academic Performance

Aggregate view of the guide’s students’ outcomes across the session: XP trends, efficiency trends, mastery progression. A guide can work hard and still have poor student outcomes — both matter.

Important: Never deliver a Triangle Evaluation rating without first having a conversation. Present findings, then ask the guide to respond.

PHASE 3 · CAMPUS DRI

Daily & Weekly Routine

From the Campus DRI Playbook. The routine below is how you structure your monitoring work each day and week.

Daily Monitoring Routine

  • Before sessions start: Open TimeBack Dash. Check yesterday’s data for students on your watchlist. Flag anyone who needs follow-up.
  • During sessions: Spot-check StudyReel recordings for anti-patterns. Note anything to follow up.
  • End of day: Log flags in your monitoring sheet. Send any urgent flags to the guide. Update your watchlist.

Weekly Routine

  • Monday: Pull weekly data from QuickSight. Set priorities for the week.
  • Tuesday–Thursday: Deep monitoring days. Review recordings, cross-reference dashboards, write any triggered Deep Dives.
  • Friday: Finalise weekly report. Conduct Triangle Evaluation (once per session). Send guide feedback. Submit to Subject DRI.
