PHASE 1 · ACCESS

Student Monitoring Platforms

Know what each platform shows and how to read it fast. This is your daily lens on student progress.

Platform Quick Reference

IXL — Primary Signal

Used for Math and ELA across most grade levels. Key metrics:

  • SmartScore: 0–100 mastery score. Target 90+ before moving on
  • Time on Task: Minutes this week vs. weekly minimum
  • Questions Attempted: Volume indicator — low = not engaging
  • Skill Path Progress: Moving forward or stuck on one skill?

Math Academy — Advanced Math

  • XP earned this week vs. target (typically 150 XP)
  • Efficiency rate — XP per minute. Below 0.5 = concern
  • Task completion — Are they completing the full path?

Mastery Track — 30-Second Overview

Cross-subject status (on track / behind / critical) aggregated from all platforms. Good for triage — then go deeper in the source platform.

Common mistake: Reading IXL SmartScore in isolation. Always pair it with time on task. A high score from one lucky session is not mastery.

PHASE 1 · CONTEXT

Company Context

Alpha's mission and model in plain language. Understanding this shapes every decision you'll make.

The Alpha Model

2-Hour Learning

Alpha students spend just 2 hours per day on academics and achieve more than traditional students spending 6–7 hours. Adaptive, mastery-based platforms combined with focused, distraction-free sessions make this possible. Your job is to protect the quality of those 2 hours.

Guides, Not Teachers

Alpha doesn't have traditional teachers. Guides are mentors who create the conditions for student autonomy. The platforms do the teaching. Guides keep students accountable, focused, and supported.

What "On Track" Means

A student is on track when they hit XP targets, spend adequate time on task, and don't exhibit anti-patterns. Two of three = monitoring. Missing all three = intervention required.

Read before day one: Alpha's Culture Doc and the Campus DRI Playbook (both in your Notion workspace).

PHASE 2 · SKILLS

Learning Science

Alpha's model is built on evidence-based principles. Understanding these helps you diagnose why students are struggling and what to do about it.

Core Principles

Mastery Before Moving On

Students don't advance until they demonstrate mastery (90+ SmartScore on IXL, equivalent on other platforms). Rushing ahead creates compounding gaps that are very hard to close later.

Spaced Repetition & Active Recall

The platforms are built on the two most evidence-backed learning strategies. When students skip content, guess randomly, or rush, they break the system and waste time.

Instructional Videos Are Not Optional

Skipping videos before questions is one of the most damaging behaviors. Students who skip consistently underperform on mastery checks and redo the same content multiple times.

Efficiency = Learning Quality

XP efficiency (XP earned ÷ time spent) is a proxy for learning quality. Low efficiency means time is being lost to wrong answers, retries, or off-task behavior. High efficiency means students are prepared, focused, and progressing cleanly.

Key insight: High time-on-task + low XP almost always means an anti-pattern problem, not a knowledge gap. Check the recording before concluding they "just don't get it."

PHASE 2 · SKILLS

Anti-Pattern Recognition

Anti-patterns are recurring behaviors that waste learning time. Spot them, name them, and get guides to address them.

The 6 Critical Anti-Patterns

Anti-PatternWhat It Looks LikeWhy It's DamagingSeverity
Video SkippingFast-forwards or skips instructional videos before questionsRemoves instruction step — repeated wrong answers, time lossCritical
Random GuessingAnswers without reading, submits instantly, doesn't check workBreaks spaced repetition; inflates wrong-answer countsCritical
App ClosingCloses app mid-lesson to restart and avoid harder contentResets progress; avoids the difficulty that drives growthCritical
Off-Task BehaviorLeaves workspace, talks to others, does non-learning tasks during sessionsInflates reported session minutes without actual learningConcern
Doom LoopStuck on same skill 3+ sessions; low SmartScore plateau with no improvementSignals knowledge gap or placement issue requiring DRI reviewConcern
Session PaddingLong sessions logged but XP/efficiency extremely lowDistorts data; guide may think student is working when they're notConcern

Documenting an Anti-Pattern

Evidence-First Rule

Never flag an anti-pattern without a recording reference. Always include:

  • Student name, grade, campus, date
  • Recording timestamp or session ID
  • Anti-pattern name (standard vocabulary above)
  • Frequency: first time / recurring / persistent
Recurring = action required. One instance = note. Two or more in the same week = write it up and flag the guide. Three or more = escalation.

PHASE 2 · SKILLS

Deep Dive Process

A structured student analysis you produce when recurring issues are detected. Your primary deliverable as Campus DRI.

Triggers

  • Student misses XP target 2+ weeks in a row
  • Two or more critical anti-patterns in one week
  • Efficiency below 60% for the second consecutive week
  • Guide flags a student or requests a review
  • Doom loop: 3+ sessions stuck on the same skill

Structure

SectionWhat Goes Here
At a GlanceKey metrics: XP, minutes, efficiency, overall status
Issues Identified1–3 specific problems with evidence references
Detailed ObservationsSupporting evidence grouped by category (Focus, Video, App Behavior)
Root CauseArea → Observed Issue → Likely Root Cause
RecommendationsOne per issue — specific, actionable, directed at Guide or DRI
Action PlanWho does what by when. Guide (this week) + DRI follow-up (end of next week)
Use the template: The Deep Dive template is in the campus-dri-playbook Notion page. Never write one from scratch.

Coaching Expectations by Role

Different guide tiers need different things from you. Know what each role requires before your first session.

Guide Tiers & Cadence

TierExperienceYour FocusCadence
New Guide0–3 monthsDaily habit formation, anti-pattern awareness, platform basicsWeekly 1:1 + daily Slack
Developing Guide3–12 monthsCoaching language, deeper student insight, handling edge casesBi-weekly 1:1
Experienced Guide12+ monthsLeadership, mentoring newer guides, complex situationsMonthly 1:1 + as needed

What Good Coaching Looks Like

Student-Facing (via the Guide)

  • Specific, evidence-based feedback — not general encouragement
  • Name the anti-pattern directly in accessible language
  • Connect behavior to outcome: "When you skip the video, here's what happens..."
  • One clear, actionable thing to change this week

Coaching the Guide

  • Share the Deep Dive summary — not just the conclusion
  • Ask before telling: "What do you think is going on with [student]?"
  • Coach them to observe and debrief, not just monitor from a distance
  • Follow up the next week to close the loop

PHASE 2 · RAMP-UP

Ramp-Up Task

Your structured first-week task. From reading about the job to actually doing it — with a safety net.

Part 1: Shadow & Observe

What to Do

Shadow a senior Campus DRI (or your manager) for a full monitoring session. Observe only.

  • Watch how they navigate the Timeback dashboard
  • Observe how they cross-reference platform data with recordings
  • Take notes on which students they flag and why
  • Ask questions after — not during

Debrief Questions

  • What was the first thing you looked at and why?
  • Which students got flagged — what was the trigger?
  • What would have been missed without the recording evidence?

Part 2: Solo Monitoring Run

What to Do

By end of Week 1, run a monitoring session on your own. Review the week's data for your assigned cohort and produce a short written summary:

  • List every student reviewed (minimum 5)
  • Flag any students needing follow-up and name the reason
  • Write up one student who would qualify for a Deep Dive trigger
  • Share with your manager for feedback before it goes anywhere else
This is a learning exercise. Your manager reviews your first solo run and gives feedback within 48 hours. Nothing you flag will be actioned until reviewed. Take risks — the point is to practice.

PHASE 3 · ROUTINES

Triangle Evaluation

Your structured assessment of guide quality. Three pillars, once per session.

The Three Pillars

Pillar 1: Guide Survey

Short structured survey sent to the guide once per session. Covers student engagement quality, intervention moments, and guide self-assessment. Read responses before delivering feedback.

Pillar 2: Head of Campus DRI Review

Your data review of the guide's students. Did their cohort improve? Were anti-patterns addressed? Were coaching points delivered? This is the quantitative backbone of the evaluation.

Pillar 3: Student Academic Performance

Aggregate view of the guide's students' outcomes: XP trends, efficiency trends, mastery progression. A guide can work hard and still have poor outcomes — both matter.

Performance Thresholds

RatingWhat It MeansAction
StrongAll three pillars positive. Students progressing, guide engaged and self-aware.Acknowledge. Document. Continue monitoring.
DevelopingMixed signals — one or two pillars show gaps.Structured coaching conversation. 2-week follow-up.
Needs SupportTwo or more pillars in the red. Student outcomes declining.Escalate to Head of Campus DRI. Formal support plan.
Important: Never deliver a Triangle Evaluation rating without first having a conversation. Present findings, then ask the guide to respond.
