09 · Campus DRI Evaluation & Feedback

Alpha · Academic DRIs · 2026–27
Updated: July 2026  ·  Owner: Head of Campus DRI

Every Campus DRI is evaluated across three quadrants. Together they give a complete picture of DRI effectiveness — from how guides experience the support they receive, to a direct review of work quality, to the academic results students actually produce. Each quadrant carries equal weight.

The 3-Quadrant Evaluation System

  Quadrant Source Frequency
1 Guide Survey Campus Guide Once per session
2 Head of Campus DRI Review Head of Campus DRI Once per session
3 Student Academic Performance TimeBack + MAP data Each MAP window + end of SY
Quadrant 1
Guide Survey
Source: Campus Guide  ·  Frequency: Once per session

Guides work alongside the Campus DRI daily and are best placed to evaluate whether the support they receive is clear, timely, and translates into better outcomes for their students.

Guides complete a short structured survey once per session. Questions cover:

  Question Scale
Q1 When the DRI flags a student concern, is it specific and actionable? Needs Improvement / Okay / Good / Excellent
Q2 Does the DRI respond to your questions and concerns promptly? Needs Improvement / Okay / Good / Excellent
Q3 Does the DRI treat you as a collaborator — not just someone to relay instructions to? Needs Improvement / Okay / Good / Excellent
Q4 Do you feel more confident supporting your students because of the DRI's input? Never / Sometimes / Mostly / Yes
Q5 Is there anything the DRI could do differently to better support you? Optional short answer
Scores are reviewed each session. Patterns matter more than individual responses. A single low score is not a flag — a consistent pattern across multiple guides is.
Quadrant 2
Head of Campus DRI Review
Source: Head of Campus DRI  ·  Frequency: Once per session

Monthly review based on campus student data, documentation quality, escalation patterns, and direct observation. This is a structured development conversation — not a performance judgment.

Scale: Needs Improvement Developing Proficient Excellent

Area What is evaluated
Monitoring quality Are reds caught early? Are false greens identified and not accepted at face value?
Root cause analysis Are interventions addressing the actual problem — or just the symptom?
Documentation accuracy Are student notes clear, specific, and up to date? Are all interventions logged?
Escalation judgment Does the DRI escalate at the right time — not too early, not too late? Are tickets complete with evidence?
Guide collaboration Are guides treated as partners? Is communication specific, non-critical, and actionable?
Student outcomes Are students on the campus progressing? Are reds being resolved, doom loops addressed, XP targets met?
Responsiveness Are red flags actioned within 24 hours? Are guide questions answered promptly?
Quadrant 3
Student Academic Performance
Source: TimeBack + MAP data  ·  Frequency: Each MAP window + end of school year

The clearest measure of a Campus DRI's impact is what students actually achieve. This quadrant looks at grade levels mastered, MAP growth, and whether students are on track for 2× growth — the core commitment of every Alpha campus.

Scale: Below Target On Target Exceeding

Metric What is evaluated
Grade levels passed per subject How many grade levels has each student mastered in Math, Reading, Language, and other subjects since the start of the year? Target: 2+ per subject per year.
MAP growth (Fall → Winter → Spring) Are students growing at the expected rate between MAP windows? Is the campus on track for 2× annual growth?
End-of-year mastery vs. start-of-year baseline For each student: where did they start, where did they finish? The delta is the most direct measure of impact.
Student outcomes are reviewed in context. A student who joined the campus severely behind, mid-year, or with attendance issues will not be evaluated the same as a student who started on Day 1. The question is always: given the circumstances, did the DRI do everything possible to move this student forward?

Performance Thresholds

Overall Standing What it means
Strong Consistently positive across both quadrants. Students are progressing, guides feel supported, and documentation reflects the work being done.
Developing One quadrant needs attention. A development conversation is initiated — focused on support, not judgment.
Concern Both quadrants show a pattern of low scores. A formal review and support plan is required.

What Campus DRIs Should Know

  • A single low guide score is not a flag — a pattern across multiple guides is.
  • The Head of Campus DRI review is not punitive. It exists to help you grow and to identify where you need support.
  • Guide feedback reflects their lived experience of being supported — not an opinion of you personally.
  • All feedback data is confidential and not shared between Campus DRIs.
  • Questions about your evaluation? Reach out to the Head of Campus DRI directly.

"The goal of evaluation is not to judge — it is to make sure every DRI has what they need to help every student succeed."
