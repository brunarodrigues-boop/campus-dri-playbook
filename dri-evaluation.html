09 · Campus DRI Evaluation & Feedback

Alpha · Academic DRIs · 2026–27
Updated: July 2026  ·  Owner: Head of Academic Operations

Every Campus DRI is evaluated across four quadrants. Together they give a complete picture of DRI effectiveness — from how guides experience the support they receive, to what the student data actually shows. Each quadrant carries equal weight.

The 4-Quadrant Evaluation System

  Quadrant Source Frequency
1 Guide Feedback Campus Guide Twice each SY
2 Automated Performance Analysis Dashboard metrics (automated) Weekly
3 Head of Academic Ops Review Head of Academic Operations Monthly
4 Student Outcomes Review MAP results + campus data Each MAP window
Quadrant 1
Guide Feedback
Source: Campus Guide  ·  Frequency: Twice each school year

Guides work alongside the Campus DRI daily. They are best placed to evaluate whether the support they receive translates into clarity, confidence, and better outcomes for their students.

Guides complete a short structured survey twice per year. Questions cover:

  • Clarity of flags: When the DRI flags a student concern, is it specific, actionable, and easy to act on?
  • Response time: Does the DRI respond to questions and concerns promptly?
  • Partnership quality: Does the DRI treat the guide as a collaborator, not just a recipient of instructions?
  • Impact on confidence: Do guides feel more confident supporting students as a result of the DRI's communication?
  • Open response: What could the DRI do differently? (Optional)
Scores are reviewed at mid-year and end of year. A single low score is not a flag — a pattern across multiple guides or both survey windows is.
Quadrant 2
Automated Performance Analysis
Source: Dashboard metrics  ·  Frequency: Weekly

Campus-level student data is reviewed automatically each week. This quadrant evaluates whether the DRI's monitoring is translating into healthy student outcomes across their roster.

Overall campus status: On Track Needs Attention At Risk

Metrics reviewed

Metric What it signals
% of students meeting weekly XP target Is the DRI catching low-output students before they fall behind?
% of red flags resolved within 24 hours Is the DRI acting on reds promptly?
Active doom loops on campus Are students stuck in test loops that have not been escalated?
Students with no activity in 48+ hours Are inactive students being caught and followed up on?
Students with open content gaps (no plan assigned) Are students waiting for content that should have been flagged?
Intervention documentation rate Are all actions logged in the student's Notes tab?
This quadrant is a reflection of daily monitoring habits. Consistent issues here indicate the DRI is not running their morning check, not acting on reds, or not documenting interventions.
Quadrant 3
Head of Academic Ops Review
Source: Head of Academic Operations  ·  Frequency: Monthly

Monthly review based on student data, documentation quality, escalation patterns, and direct observation. This is not a punitive review — it is a structured conversation about what is working and what needs support.

Scale: Needs Improvement Developing Proficient Excellent

Area What is evaluated
Monitoring quality Are reds caught early? Are false greens identified and not accepted at face value?
Root cause analysis Are interventions addressing the actual problem — or just the symptom?
Documentation accuracy Are student notes clear, specific, and up to date? Are all interventions logged?
Escalation judgment Does the DRI escalate at the right time — not too early, not too late? Are tickets complete?
Guide collaboration Are guides treated as partners? Is communication specific, non-critical, and actionable?
Responsiveness Are reds actioned within 24 hours? Are guide questions answered promptly?
Quadrant 4
Student Outcomes Review
Source: MAP results + campus data  ·  Frequency: Each MAP window

Ultimately, the Campus DRI is accountable for student academic outcomes. This quadrant evaluates whether the DRI's daily monitoring, interventions, and escalations have translated into measurable progress.

Scale: Below Target On Target Exceeding

Area What is evaluated
MAP growth Are students on track for 2× grade-level growth? How does the campus compare to target?
Grade levels mastered How many grade levels have students advanced since the start of the year per subject?
XP target hit rate What % of students are consistently meeting their weekly XP and time targets?
Struggling students How many students remain below 60% accuracy or stuck in doom loops? Were they escalated?
Placement accuracy Are all students working at the right level? Any placements that should have been escalated earlier?
Student outcomes are influenced by many factors beyond the DRI's control. This quadrant is reviewed in context — the question is whether the DRI's actions were appropriate given the circumstances, not solely whether numbers are green.

Performance Thresholds

Overall Standing What it means
Strong Consistently positive across all 4 quadrants. Students are progressing, guides feel supported, documentation is clean, and outcomes reflect the effort.
Developing One or two quadrants need attention. A development conversation is initiated — not punitive, but structured.
Concern A pattern of low scores across multiple quadrants. A formal review and support plan is required.

What Campus DRIs Should Know

  • A single low automated metric is not a flag — a pattern across multiple weeks is.
  • The Automated Performance Analysis and Head of Academic Ops Review are not punitive. They exist to help you improve and identify where you need support.
  • Guide feedback reflects their experience of being supported — not their opinion of you personally.
  • Student outcomes are reviewed in context. A student who was severely behind when you took over will not be held against you if you acted correctly.
  • All feedback data is confidential and not shared between Campus DRIs.
  • Questions about your evaluation? Reach out to the Head of Academic Operations directly.

"The goal of evaluation is not to judge — it is to make sure every DRI has what they need to help every student succeed."
