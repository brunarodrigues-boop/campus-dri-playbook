Purpose

The Campus DRI is responsible for ensuring every student is progressing academically while supporting guides through data interpretation, placements, testing, and interventions. The goal is simple:

  • Students are always working at the appropriate level.
  • Academic issues are identified early.
  • Guides know exactly how to support students.
  • Parents receive accurate information.
  • Escalations happen before they become problems.
Role scope: Minimum hours 8:00 AM – 12:00 PM CT. You are the single point of academic accountability for your campus. You do not wait to be assigned students — you proactively find issues before guides or students ask.

Success Criteria

You are doing your job when your campus achieves all three of the following:

02 · The Student Journey (TimeBack)

Understanding where a student is in the system is your first step before any intervention.

Onboarding → First Test

Students start by completing MAP tests (handled by Thiago De Sa and Guilherme Colombo). Once MAPs are done, students receive their first subject test:

  • Below 6th grade age-grade → starts with the Kinder (K) test
  • 6th grade or above → starts with the 3rd grade test

Bracketing Logic

ScoreNext Step
Below 60% (below 80% for K–3 Math)Full skill plan
60–90%Hole filling
Over 90%Next grade test
First Test Exception: If the student's first test is not a Kinder test and they fail it, they receive a Kinder test next before entering the normal path. This applies to the first test only.
Example: First test is 3rd grade, student gets 80% → next they get a Kinder test, then proceed upward.

What to Check Daily

Test Out Sheet

  • Score below 90%? → Confirm hole filling or full plan was assigned. If not → contact Curriculum DRI.
  • Score over 90%? → Confirm next grade test was assigned. If not → request via the test out sheet.

Reading Tests

  • 100-for-100 students get 3 attempts at the reading test.
  • 3 failed attempts → evaluate for MobyMax Reading or AlphaRead. Confirm with Aleksandra Wrega (Alex).
  • HS students (age-grade 9–12) on Grades 3–8 AlphaRead → contact Alex to manually lower the level to their rostered grade.
  • Student below 60% on Grade K, 1, or 2 reading test → request Lalilo account from Nick Alsford.

Plan / Hole Filling Completion

  • Most is automated. If a student has run out of content, check their TimeBack and apps, then contact the Curriculum DRI to assign more.

Placement Process

Confirm students are working at the correct grade level, receiving appropriate placement tests, and progressing normally through placement pathways. If placement seems incorrect:

  1. Verify completed coursework.
  2. Review previous assessment data.
  3. Confirm testing sequence.
  4. Escalate when necessary.
Never request unnecessary placement changes without evidence.

Assessment Support

Before requesting or assigning tests, verify:

  • Student completed prerequisite work.
  • Benchmarks have been met.
  • Student is academically ready.
  • Testing is appropriate for transcript needs.

Examples: Alpha placements · FastMath benchmarks · Reading placements · Math placements · Grade-level assessments.

03 · Daily Monitoring Routine

Morning Check

Open TimeBack Dash and review your campus student list. Prioritise by urgency, not by order. Flag any student where:

  • XP or time on task is significantly below expected
  • A student is cycling the same test without passing (doom loop)
  • No activity in 24+ hours on a school day
  • Accuracy is below 60% across recent sessions
  • Follow-up from a previous intervention is due

What to Evaluate Per Student

Time

  • Daily time on task
  • Weekly consistency
  • Trends over time

Productivity

  • Productive vs. unproductive time
  • Time spent idle
  • Excessive app switching
  • Long inactive periods

Academic Progress

  • Lessons completed
  • XP earned
  • Accuracy
  • Pace of progression
  • Placement movement

Anti-Patterns to Watch

Anti-PatternWhat It Looks Like in TimeBack
Rushing through contentShort session times relative to XP earned; high attempt rate with low dwell time
Skipping explanations after errorsWrong answer followed immediately by next attempt with no dwell on explanation
Guessing / clicking throughVery short response times; high attempt count, low accuracy
Skipping instructional materialQuiz attempts with no lesson view beforehand
Idling / off-taskLong gaps of inactivity mid-session; session time logged without activity
Gaming the systemRetaking easy or already-mastered content for XP; cycling low-level content repeatedly
When you spot an anti-pattern: Note it in the student's Notes tab → Flag the guide in Slack with the specific data point → Recommend a coaching session → If the pattern repeats, escalate to the Subject DRI and flag in the weekly report.

Intervention Decision Tree

Student is behind on time

Diagnose: Attendance issue? Motivation? Technical problem? Doesn't understand expectations?

Action: Coach guide · Contact family if needed · Build recovery plan.

Student has low productivity

Diagnose: Distracted? Constant tab switching? Off-task?

Action: Review habits · Coach guide · Schedule observation if needed.

Student progressing slowly

Diagnose: Knowledge gap? Reading comprehension? Confidence? Avoiding difficult work?

Action: Flag for coaching session · Scaffold learning · Set small achievable goals.

Student progressing too quickly

Diagnose: Guessing? Skipping explanations? Rushing assessments?

Action: Slow them down before accelerating. Flag guide.

Weekly Analysis

At least once per week, step back from daily monitoring and evaluate each student's trajectory. The question is not just "what happened today" but "are they on track for their next milestone?"

If weekly goals were met

  • Confirm the student is on track for the next MAP or assessment milestone
  • Look for opportunities to accelerate — are they ready for the next test?
  • Document wins to share with guide and family

If weekly goals were not met

  • Identify root cause before acting: placement? attendance? engagement? technical issue? knowledge gap?
  • Adjust the plan: add content, reassign, contact DRI, or escalate
  • Set a revised target for the following week — don't leave it open-ended
  • Document the change and notify the guide
A student who misses their weekly goal two weeks in a row requires an escalation note, not just a flag. Patterns need action.

Rules to Stand By

These are non-negotiable operating principles for the Campus DRI role.

#RuleWhat it means in practice
1Use apps first-handLog in as a student. Experience the platform before advising on it. Never diagnose what you haven't seen yourself.
2Own the outcomeYou are accountable for student progress — not just for monitoring it. If a student is stuck, it is your responsibility to move them forward.
3Run a single decluttered trackerOne source of truth. Scattered notes and informal tracking create gaps. Everything goes in the same place.
4Spot false greensGreen metrics can mask real problems — rushing, guessing, gaming XP. Verify activity quality, not just quantity.
5Act within 24 hours on redsAny red flag — no activity, repeated test failures, zero XP — gets a documented action within one business day.
6Guide communication is collaborativeBring guides into your analysis. They are your partner, not just the recipients of your flags.
7Log every issueIf it's not documented, it didn't happen. Undocumented interventions cannot be escalated, tracked, or reviewed.

Deep Dive Process

During student reviews, structure your analysis around four areas:

Wins

Growth · Consistency · Strong habits

Concerns

Time · Productivity · Accuracy · Behaviour patterns

Root Cause

Avoid treating symptoms. Ask: Why is this happening? Common root causes: reading comprehension · motivation · missing prerequisite knowledge · attendance · technical issues.

Action Plan

Every Deep Dive ends with clear next steps:

  • Student action
  • Guide action
  • Campus DRI follow-up
  • Timeline for review

04 · Subject-Specific Support

These are the most common issues to watch for per subject when reviewing student data or flagging students for intervention.

Reading

Watch for: skipping instructions · guessing · reading only part of the question · vocabulary gaps.

When flagging or coaching a guide: point students toward reading twice, highlighting key information, and explaining the question before answering.

Math

Common issues in the data: misreading questions · ignoring units · missing keywords · place value confusion · struggling with multi-step problems · no visible working.

Interventions should focus on reasoning, not computation — breaking problems apart, identifying what's known and unknown.

Science

Watch for: vocabulary misunderstandings · difficulty interpreting diagrams · lack of background knowledge.

Interventions: direct students to read captions, look at visuals first, and use process of elimination.

05 · Guide Support & Parent Communication

Coaching Guides

When flagging issues to a guide, lead with observations, not criticism. Be collaborative.

Instead of: "Your student isn't learning."

Use: "I noticed they're answering quickly without reading explanations. Let's try slowing them down and asking them to explain their thinking before submitting."

Parent Communication

Keep all communication honest, specific, and solution-oriented. Always include:

  • What was observed
  • Why it matters
  • What is being done
  • How the family can help

Avoid educational jargon.

06 · Escalation Guidelines

When to Escalate

  • Student cannot progress due to platform issues
  • Placement appears incorrect
  • Testing requests fall outside normal processes
  • Technical problems prevent learning
  • Student requires curriculum review
  • Parent concern requires additional support

How to Escalate

Always escalate with: student name · campus · clear summary · evidence · requested action.

Best practices: Look for trends, not isolated events. Verify before escalating. Document every intervention. Follow up after action is taken.
