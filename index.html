Your Role as Campus DRI

You are the single point of academic accountability for your assigned campus. This is not a passive monitoring role — you own the student experience end-to-end, from flagging a student stuck in a doom loop on Monday to having an adjusted plan in place before they start Tuesday. You do not wait to be assigned students. You proactively find the students who need help before they — or their guide — asks.

Timeback
The Student Journey

Understanding where a student is in the system is your first step before any intervention. Every student follows the same path — your job is to know exactly where they are at any moment.

Onboarding → First Test

Students start by completing their MAP tests (handled by Thiago De Sa and Guilherme Colombo). Once MAPs are done, students receive their first test per subject:

  • Below 6th grade age-grade → starts with the Kinder (K) test
  • 6th grade or above → starts with the 3rd grade test

Bracketing Logic

ScoreNext Step
Below 60% (below 80% for K–3 Math)Full skill plan
60–90%Hole filling
Over 90%Next grade test
ℹ
First Test Exception

If the student's first test is not a Kinder test and they fail it, they receive a Kinder test next before entering the normal path. This applies to the first test only.
Example: Student's first test is 3rd grade, they get 80% → next they get a Kinder test, then proceed upward.

What to Check Daily

Test Out Sheet

  • Score below 90%? → Confirm hole filling or full plan was assigned. If not → contact the Curriculum DRI.
  • Score over 90%? → Confirm the next grade test was assigned. If not → request via the test out sheet.

Reading Tests

  • Reading is an exception. 100-for-100 students get 3 attempts at the reading test.
  • 3 failed attempts → evaluate for MobyMax Reading or AlphaRead. Confirm with Aleksandra Wrega (Alex).
  • HS students (age-grade 9–12) placed on Grades 3–8 AlphaRead → reach out to Alex to manually lower AlphaRead level to their rostered grade.
  • Student below 60% on Grade K, 1, or 2 reading test → request a Lalilo account from Nick Alsford.

Plan / Hole Filling Completion

  • Most is automated. You will receive requests from students who've run out of content.
  • Check their TimeBack and apps. If content is genuinely missing → contact the Curriculum DRI to assign more.
03
Every Morning
Daily Monitoring Routine
  1. Open TimeBack Dash. Review all assigned students. Flag anyone where XP or time on task is significantly below expected, test attempts are stacking without a pass (doom loop), or there is no activity in 24+ hours on a school day.
  2. Cross-reference with QuickSight. For any flagged student, open the Student Deep Dives Dashboard. Look for grade-level progress stall (no advancement in 2+ weeks), retake patterns on the same content, and Missing Assignment Detection flags.
  3. Check the System Failure Tracker. Are there any system failures affecting your campus? Missing plans, wrong enrolments, tests not assigned? Log and escalate to BeyondAI before the student's session starts.
  4. Review the Test Out Sheet. Were any tests completed yesterday? Did automation fire the correct next step?
  5. Flag and act. System issue → escalate immediately. Learning issue or anti-pattern → queue a coaching session or notify the guide. Doom loop → run or assign a targeted session focused on the blocking skill.

Anti-Patterns to Watch

These behaviours look productive but do not lead to actual learning. Campus DRIs catch them at the data level — before the student or guide notices.

Anti-PatternWhat It Looks Like in TimeBack
Rushing through contentShort session times relative to XP earned; high attempt rate with low dwell time
Skipping explanations after errorsWrong answer followed immediately by next attempt with no dwell time on explanation
Guessing / clicking throughVery short response times across many questions; high attempt count, low accuracy
Skipping instructional materialQuiz attempts with no lesson view beforehand
Idling / off-taskLong gaps of inactivity mid-session; session time logged without activity
Gaming the systemRetaking easy/already-mastered content for XP; cycling low-level content repeatedly

During the Session — The LIFT Framework

L
Locate the Block
Use powerful questions to find the root cause — not just the symptom. Ask "Where exactly does it break down for you?" before offering any support.
I
Illuminate the Path
Guide the student to discover the solution through leading questions, analogies, and worked examples. The coach narrates thinking, not answers.
F
Foster Independence
Encourage the student to attempt the next problem independently. Coach observes, resists the urge to intervene, and celebrates the attempt.
T
Transfer Ownership
Close with a student-led summary: "Explain back to me what you'd do next time." Building metacognitive habits that outlast any single session.
★
Two Critical Goals of Every Session

Unblock the student — correct the misunderstanding behind the skill they're struggling with.
Build their independence — teach them to use in-app resources so they can master future skills on their own.

Coaching Rules

  • Use only leading questions — never give the answer directly
  • In Math: follow the lesson's prescribed method; do not teach your own approach
  • Reduce help as the session progresses → end with zero support on the final questions
  • Tailor to the error type: Conceptual (misunderstanding the idea) · Procedural (wrong steps) · Careless (misreading)
  • Watch for uncertainty signals ("I guess," "is this the step?") → slow down, ask for a confidence rating
  • If the student doesn't arrive: wait 5 min → ping guide in #guides-and-coaches-2hourlearning → wait 5 more min → mark absent and end call
!
Non-Negotiable

The student must complete the last few questions with zero support. Mastery only counts if the student can demonstrate it independently.

Blocker Field Guide — Row 16

This data is how the team finds and fixes gaps in the system. Row 17 must always match Row 16.

Blocker
When to use it
Missing Instruction
Instruction that should exist simply doesn't. A concept was never taught, a video/text is missing, or a learning resource is genuinely absent.
Faulty Instruction
The content or instructions given were incorrect, confusing, or misleading — actively wrong in a way that caused the confusion.
Prerequisites
The student hasn't learned the foundational skills needed for this lesson. Content is fine — student just isn't ready for it yet.
Placement Failure
The student was placed at the wrong level — too easy or too hard. The mismatch itself caused the issue, not the content quality.
Too Large a Conceptual Leap
Instruction exists and is correct, but the jump between what the student knew and what they were asked to do was too big.
Other
Doesn't fit any above. Use Row 17 to explain clearly. Covers app UI/navigation issues, coachable student behaviour anti-patterns, or unique situations.
None
No meaningful blocker — the student resolved the issue on their own. Should be the exception, not the default.

Contacts
Always / Never & Evaluation

✓ Always

  • Be calm, polite, patient, and encouraging
  • Guide using leading questions only
  • Encourage students to use in-app tools
  • Gradually reduce help toward full independence
  • Point out and correct misconceptions
  • Adapt to the student's individual needs
  • Celebrate wins
  • Document every session the same day
  • Escalate system issues — never absorb them silently

✗ Never

  • Give away answers (except worked examples)
  • Interrupt a student's thought process
  • Help while the student is working independently
  • Lecture — if you must explain, make it a conversation
  • Drag out the session unnecessarily
  • Leave Row 16 blank or mismatched with Row 17
  • Mark a system issue as "None" — escalate it
  • Wait more than 24 hrs to document a session

Common Situations & FAQ
There are no recommendations available for a student
  • Just completed a test: Recommendations run once a day. Knowledge gaps will appear the next day.
  • Just completed their knowledge gaps: Check DeepDive Dash for when the last lesson was completed. More content will appear the next cycle.
  • No reading content, rostered in K–2: They may need a Lalilo account. Reach out to Nick Alsford.
  • None of the above: Immediate support ticket.
MobyMax — no recommendations available
Check the student's MobyMax link and order by latest completed. Confirm if lessons were completed with over 80%. If not automatically reassigned within 15 min, reassign manually:
  • Open the student's MobyMax link → find the course to reassign (e.g., Language HF)
  • Sort lessons by accuracy → select all lessons below 80%
  • Click "Assign Remediation Skills" → name it "Reassign Hole-Filling. [Subject] [Student Name]"
  • Wait 15 minutes before doing this manually — always prefer automation.
Freckle (Language specifically): Check the student's Freckle account. If empty, ping Barbara Franks.
The student wants to test out of their grade
  • Doing knowledge gaps: Explain that knowledge gaps must be completed before a new test is assigned.
  • Rostered, scored below 60%: Explain the student has many gaps and would benefit from the full plan. Confirm with Curriculum DRI before making any change.
  • Rostered in K–3 Math, scored 60–80%: Discuss with the DRI whether to use Zearn placements from G3 or move forward in Zearn missions (K–2).
The student is getting repeated lessons
  • Full plan: Check if all lessons for that grade were completed — there may have been a bug that reassigned the plan from the start.
  • Knowledge gaps: If they get a repeated lesson after a test, it usually means they failed the same type of lesson again in the test.
The student has the test in TimeBack but not in Alphatest
Usually means the test was marked as absent. Double-check in Alphatest and open a support ticket to reassign the test.
The student is rostered but failed the test on purpose
Go to Guilherme Colombo / Thiago De Sa and ask for a test to be manually added. These students must be tracked separately.
HS student placed on Grades 3–8 AlphaRead
If a student's age-grade is 9–12 and they are placed on Grades 3–8 AlphaRead, reach out to Aleksandra Wrega (Alex) to manually lower the AlphaRead level to the grade they were rostered on.

Appendix
High-Mileage Questions

Use these during sessions to diagnose blockers and unlock student thinking. Match the question set to the situation — don't cycle through all of them.

Missing Instruction
  • What do you feel like you needed to know before this that you didn't?
  • Is there a step that feels like it came out of nowhere?
  • What would have made this make sense from the beginning?
Prerequisites
  • Where was the last place this felt easy for you?
  • Can you do [simpler version]? Let's start there.
  • What part of this feels familiar and what part feels completely new?
Too Large a Conceptual Leap
  • What part is confusing to you?
  • Can you tell me what you DO understand so far?
  • What's the first place it stopped making sense?
Mindset / Fixed Mindset
  • What would you try if you knew you couldn't fail?
  • Has there been something else that felt impossible at first that you figured out?
  • What's one small thing you do understand about this?
Learned Helplessness
  • Before I say anything, what's one thing you could try?
  • What have you already attempted?
  • If I wasn't here, what would your first move be?
Disengagement
  • What would make this feel worth your time?
  • Where do you see this connecting to something you really care about?
  • What would make this more interesting to you right now?
Dysregulated / Emotionally Blocked
  • What would help you feel ready to work on this right now?
  • On a scale of 1–10, how focused do you feel today?
  • What do you need before we start?
